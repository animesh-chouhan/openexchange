Nice, this is where you make the talk feel alive instead of a lecture.

You want questions that:

- warm them up
- connect to their experience
- set up your concepts

Here’s a **tight sequence you can actually use live**.

---

# 🔹 Warm-up (get hands up)

Start simple.

**“Quick show of hands…”**

- Who here has ever traded stocks or crypto?
- Who has placed at least one BUY order?
- Who has no idea how this works under the hood?

👉 This helps you calibrate the room instantly.

---

# 🔹 Hook Questions (create curiosity)

Right after:

- When you pressed BUY… did you think about where that order goes?
- Do you think your order directly goes to the stock exchange?

Pause here.

Then say:

**“It doesn’t.”**

That line lands.

---

# 🔹 Intuition Questions (lead into concepts)

Before explaining matching:

- If I want to buy at ₹100 and someone wants to sell at ₹105… should a trade happen?
- What if I’m willing to pay ₹105 and someone is selling at ₹100?

Let them answer.

Then drop:

**“That one inequality runs the entire market.”**

---

# 🔹 Order Book Interaction (fun + memorable)

Turn audience into the exchange:

Say:

> “Let’s simulate a market.”

Pick 2–3 people:

- You → “Buy 100 at 101”
- You → “Buy 100 at 100”
- You → “Sell 100 at 99”

Then ask the room:

**“Who should get matched first?”**

Let them think.

Then reveal:

- highest buyer matches first
- lowest seller wins

👉 This makes order book logic stick permanently.

---

# 🔹 Data Structure Setup

Before heaps slide:

- If I keep getting thousands of orders… how do I quickly find:
  - highest buyer?
  - lowest seller?

Let them say:

- sorting
- searching

Then you say:

**“We need something faster → heaps.”**

---

# 🔹 Demo Build-up

Right before your demo:

- What do you think will happen if I run this?
- Will all orders match immediately?
- Will some remain pending?

Now they’re invested in the output.

---

# 🔹 Scaling Curiosity

Before infra slide:

- How fast do you think real exchanges operate?
  - milliseconds?
  - microseconds?
  - nanoseconds?

Then reveal:

👉 microseconds (and even lower in HFT)

---

# 🔹 Closing Reflection

End with something reflective:

- After this, will you look at the BUY button differently?
- What surprised you the most?

---

# 🔹 One killer question (don’t skip this)

Ask slowly:

> “If two people send the same order at the exact same time… who gets priority?”

Let them think.

Then say:

**“That question alone has shaped billions of dollars of trading infrastructure.”**

# A Subtle but Powerful Question

If two people send the exact same order at the exact same time…

**Who gets priority?**

---

### Think about it

- same price
- same quantity
- same timestamp (to the millisecond?)

**So… who wins?**

---

### The Answer

The one who arrives _first_.

But…

**What does “first” even mean?**

---

### Why this matters

- nanoseconds decide priority
- firms spend millions to be faster
- servers are placed physically closer to exchanges

---

### Big Idea

**Fairness in markets = how you define time and ordering.**

---

### One Line

This single question has shaped billions of dollars of trading infrastructure.
